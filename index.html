🏆 1st Place — Inaugural Frog Tank
Product Strategy & Execution · Fidelity Investments · Dec 2024
Finny & Flo

Led Team Big Hoppers through Fidelity’s internal Shark Tank-style innovation challenge — 18 presentations, 2 rounds, one winning team. Designed and pitched a gamified financial literacy concept for middle schoolers to a panel of senior executives.

The Problem

Gen Z and middle school students enter adulthood without foundational financial literacy, leading to poor money habits, high debt, and long-term brand distrust for financial institutions. Existing tools are too dry and too abstract to engage young users.

My Approach

I led the Big Hoppers through the full product lifecycle: discovery research to identify core literacy gaps, competitive analysis of EdTech and FinTech onboarding flows, rapid prototyping of Finny & Flo as a gamified board game concept. I scoped the MVP around three core literacy gaps instead of ten — because a focused product wins against a feature-bloated one. Managed team alignment under tight deadlines, defined user journeys, and built the high-fidelity pitch deck for executive leadership.

Key Tradeoff

Resisted pressure to add complex digital features. Kept the MVP as a physical board game to maximize engagement with middle schoolers and reduce friction for in-classroom adoption — a deliberate scope decision that strengthened our pitch.

Outcome
1st Place — Inaugural Frog Tank Winner. Selected from 18 presentations across 2 rounds. Recognized by Fidelity’s senior leadership as a differentiated, feasible approach to Gen Z financial literacy and long-term brand engagement.
Product Builder · Nov 2025
Product Design & Build · Decision Support · FinTech
Trading Alpha

Most retail traders fail not because of bad strategy — but because of emotional decisions under pressure. I identified three root causes and built a decision support system that enforces discipline before a single order is placed.

The Problem

Three root causes of retail trading failure: Emotional Bias (revenge trades, FOMO after losses), Risk Fragmentation (manual position sizing leads to oversizing errors), and Lack of Pre-Trade Structure (no checklist to confirm macro and sector alignment before entry). Existing tools journaled results — none intervened at the point of decision.

Product Decisions & Scope

I scoped Trading Alpha as a Decision Support System, not a journal. MVP prioritized four features mapped directly to the three root causes: an Alpha Protocol Wizard that scores each trade across Fundamentals, Technicals, and Sentiment — flagging trades below threshold before execution. A Volatility-Tiered Scaling engine that dynamically adjusts risk per trade. A Risk Runway gauge that makes capital depletion tangible. And a Mental Check-In that triggers a lockout when the trader is emotionally compromised.

Design choice: high-density terminal aesthetic to reduce visual noise and keep the trader calm under pressure.

Technical Execution

Built as a PWA in React 19 + TypeScript for mobile-ready access from day one. Custom risk engine developed in-house. Shipped solo as a fully functional product.

Outcome
Fully functional PWA shipped as a solo product builder. Eliminated overtrading via hard stops and risk limits. Quantified strategy edge by tracking Protocol Scores alongside PnL. Delivered Calendar Heatmap and Equity Curve for session performance analysis.
MAC REAL STUDIOS
Product Lead · Software Studio · Google Play
Product Design & Build · Wear OS · Google Play
Mac Real Studios

Independent product studio — full ownership from gap identification to App Store distribution. First product: Strobe Light For Watch, a Wear OS utility solving a real visibility problem for night runners. Live on Google Play.

Visit Studio
The Problem

Wear OS had a clear gap: night runners and outdoor users who need to be visible have almost no purpose-built options. Existing alternatives require pulling out a phone or wearing bulky accessories — an underserved niche with a simple, solvable need.

Key Product Decision

I scoped a single-purpose interface: one tap to activate, no configuration required. The critical tradeoff was resisting feature creep — color modes, SOS patterns, sync features were all cut. The user is mid-run in the dark. Simplicity is the product.

Outcome
Live on Google Play. Full product ownership from gap identification to distribution. Using user reviews to inform the v2 roadmap. Additional productivity tools in development — each scoped around the same philosophy: identify a real gap, build the simplest product that solves it, ship it.
